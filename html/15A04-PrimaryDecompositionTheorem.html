primary decomposition theorem


This is an important theorem in linear algebra. It states the following: Let k be a field, V a vector space over k, dim⁡V=n, and T:V→V a linear operator, such that its minimal polynomial (or its annihilator polynomial) is mT, which decomposes in k⁢[X] into irreducible factors as mT=p1α1⁢…⁢prαr. Then,

  1. 1.

    V=⊕i=1rker⁡(piαi⁢(T))

  2. 2.

    ker⁡(piαi⁢(T)) is T-invariant for every i

  3. 3.

    If Ti is the restriction of T to ker⁡(piαi⁢(T)), then mTi=piαi

This is a consequence of a more general theorem: Let V, T be as above, and f∈k⁢[X] such that f⁢(T)=0, with f=f1⁢…⁢fr and (fi,fj)=1 if i≠j, then

  1. 1.

    V=⊕i=1rker⁡(fi⁢(T))

  2. 2.

    ker⁡(fi⁢(T)) is T-invariant for every i

To illustrate its importance, the primary decomposition theorem, together with the cyclic decomposition theorem, imply the existence and uniqueness of the Jordan canonical form.
