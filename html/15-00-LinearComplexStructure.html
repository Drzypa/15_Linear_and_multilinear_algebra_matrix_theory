linear complex structure


A on a real vector space V, with dim⁢(V)=m, is a linear automorphism J∈Aut⁢(V) such that J2=J∘J=-idV. With a complex structure J we can consider V as a complex vector space with the product ℂ×V→V given by

(x+i⁢y)⁢𝐯=x⁢𝐯+y⁢J⁢(𝐯),∀x,y∈ℝ,𝐯∈V.

This implies that the dimension m of V must be even.

A common example is V=ℝ2⁢n with the standard basis 𝐞1,…,𝐞n,𝐟1,…,𝐟n, for which we can obtain a complex structure J0∈Aut⁢(ℝ2⁢n) represented by the matrix

(𝟎𝐈n-𝐈n𝟎).

Here 𝐈n∈Mn⁢(ℝ) is the identity n×n matrix and 𝟎∈Mn⁢(ℝ) is the zero n×n matrix.
