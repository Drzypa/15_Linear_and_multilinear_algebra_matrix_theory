matrix representation of a bilinear form


Given a bilinear form, B:U×V→K, we show how we can represent it with a matrix, with respect to a particular pair of bases for U and V

Suppose U and V are finite-dimensional and we have chosen bases, ℬ1={e1,…} and ℬ2={f1,…}. Now we define the matrix C with entries Ci⁢j=B⁢(ei,fj). This will be the matrix associated to B with respect to this basis as follows; If we write x,y∈V as column vectors in terms of the chosen bases, then check B⁢(x,y)=xT⁢C⁢y. Further if we choose the corresponding dual bases for U∗ and V∗ then C and CT are the corresponding matrices for BR and BL, respectively (in the sense of linear maps). Thus we see that a symmetric bilinear form is represented by a symmetric matrix, and similarly for skew-symmetric forms.

Let ℬ1′ and ℬ2′ be new bases, and P and Q the corresponding change of basis matrices. Then the new matrix is C′=PT⁢C⁢Q.
