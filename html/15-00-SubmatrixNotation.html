submatrix notation


Let n and k be integers with 1≤k≤n. Denote by Qk,n the totality of all sequences of k integers, where the elements of the sequence are strictly increasing and choosen from {1,…,n}.

Let A=(ai⁢j) be an m×n matrix with elements from some set, usually taken to be a field for ring. Let k and r be positive integers with 1≤k≤m, 1≤r≤n, α∈Qk,m and β∈Qr,n. We let α=(i1,…,ik) and β=(j1,…,jr)

The submatrix A⁢[α,β] has (s,t) entry equal to ais⁢jt and has k rows and r columns.

We denote by A⁢(α,β) the submatrix of A whose rows and columns are complementary to α and β, respectively.

We can also define similarly the notations A⁢[α,β) and A⁢(α,β].
