right-handed system of vectors


Three ordered non-coplanar vectors u→, v→ and w→, which have a common , are said to form a right-handed or dextral system (Latin dexter = ), if a right-threaded screw rotated through an angle less than 180o from u→ to v→ will advance in the direction of w→.  For instance, the usual basis vectors i→, j→ and k→ form a dextral system.
