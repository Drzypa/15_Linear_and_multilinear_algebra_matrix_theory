dimension theorem for symplectic complement (proof)


We denote by V⋆ the dual space of V, i.e., linear mappings from V to ℝ. Moreover, we assume known that dim⁡V=dim⁡V∗ for any vector space V.

We begin by showing that the mapping S:V→V*, a↦ω⁢(a,⋅) is an linear isomorphism. First, linearity is clear, and since ω is non-degenerate, ker⁡S={0}, so S is injective. To show that S is surjective, we apply the http://planetmath.org/node/2238rank-nullity theorem to S, which yields dim⁡V=dim⁡imgS. We now have imgS⊂V* and dim⁡imgS=dim⁡V∗. (The first assertion follows directly from the definition of S.) Hence imgS=V∗ (see this page (http://planetmath.org/VectorSubspace)), and S is a surjection. We have shown that S is a linear isomorphism.

Let us next define the mapping T:V→W*, a↦ω⁢(a,⋅). Applying the http://planetmath.org/node/2238rank-nullity theorem to T yields

dim⁡V = dim⁡ker⁡T+dim⁡imgT. (1)

Now ker⁡T=Wω and imgT=W*. To see the latter assertion, first note that from the definition of T, we have imgT⊂W*. Since S is a linear isomorphism, we also have imgT⊃W*. Then, since dim⁡W=dim⁡W*, the result follows from equation 1. □
