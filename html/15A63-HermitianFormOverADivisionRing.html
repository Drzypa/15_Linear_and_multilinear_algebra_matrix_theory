Hermitian form over a division ring


Let D be a division ring admitting an involution (http://planetmath.org/Involution2) *. Let V be a vector space over D. A Hermitian form over D is a function from V×V to D, denoted by (⋅,⋅) with the following properties, for any v,w∈V and d∈D:

  1. 1.

    (⋅,⋅) is additive in each of its arguments,

  2. 2.

    (d⁢u,v)=d⁢(u,v),

  3. 3.

    (u,d⁢v)=(u,v)⁢d*,

  4. 4.

    (u,v)=(v,u)*.

Note that if the Hermitian form (⋅,⋅) is non-trivial and if * is the identity on D, then D is a field and (⋅,⋅) is just a symmetric bilinear form.

If we replace the last condition by (u,v)=-(v,u)*, then (⋅,⋅) over D is called a skew Hermitian form.

Remark. Every skew Hermitian form over a division ring induces a Hermitian form and vice versa.
