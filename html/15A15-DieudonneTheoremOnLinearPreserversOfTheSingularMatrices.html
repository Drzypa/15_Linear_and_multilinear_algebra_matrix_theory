Dieudonné theorem on linear preservers of the singular matrices


Let 𝔽 be an arbitrary field. Consider ℳn⁢(𝔽), the vector space of all n×n matrices over 𝔽. Moreover, let 𝒢⁢ℒn⁢(𝔽) be the full linear group of nonsingular n×n matrices over 𝔽.

Theorem 1.

For a linear automorphism φ:Mn⁢(F)⟶Mn⁢(F) the following conditions are equivalent:
(i) ∀A∈Mn⁢(F):det⁡(A)=0⇒det⁡(φ⁢(A))=0, (ii) either ∃P,Q∈G⁢Ln⁢(F)⁢∀A∈Mn⁢(F):φ⁢(A)=P⁢A⁢Q, or ∃P,Q∈G⁢Ln⁢(F)⁢∀A∈Mn⁢(F):φ⁢(A)=P⁢A⊤⁢Q.

The original proof [D] of the nontrivial implication (i) ⇒ (ii) is based on the fundamental theorem of projective geometry.
