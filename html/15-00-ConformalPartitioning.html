conformal partitioning


Let R be a ring. Let the matrices A∈Mm,n⁢(R) and B∈Mn,p⁢(R) be partitioned into submatrices Ai,j and Bi,j respectively as follows:

A=n1n2⋯nh[A1,1⏞A1,2⏞⋯A1,h⏞A2,1A2,2⋯A2,h⋮⋮⋮Ag,1Ag,2⋯Ag,h]}m1}m2⋮}mg

where Ai,j is mi×nj,∑i=1gmi=m, ∑j=1hnj=n;

B=p1p2⋯pk[B1,1⏞B1,2⏞⋯B1,k⏞B2,1B2,2⋯B2,k⋮⋮⋮Bh,1Bh,2⋯Bh,k]}n1}n2⋮}nh

where Bi,j is ni×pj, ∑j=1kpj=p. Then A and B (in this ) are said to be conformally partitioned for multiplication.

Now suppose that A and B are conformally partitioned for multiplication. Let C=A⁢B be partitioned as follows:

C=p1p2⋯pk[C1,1⏞C1,2⏞⋯C1,k⏞C2,1C2,2⋯C2,k⋮⋮⋮Cg,1Cg,2⋯Cg,k]}m1}m2⋮}mg

where Ci,j is mi×pj, i=1,⋯,g, j=1,⋯,k. Then

Ci,j=∑t=1kAi,t⁢Bt,j,i=1,⋯,g,j=1,⋯,k.

This method of computing A⁢B is sometimes called block multiplication.
