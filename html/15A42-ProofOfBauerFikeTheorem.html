proof of Bauer-Fike theorem


We can assume λ~∉σ⁢(A) (otherwise, we can choose λ=λ~ and theorem is proven, since κp⁢(X)>1). Then (A-λ~⁢I)-1 exists, so we can write:

u~=(A-λ~⁢I)-1⁢r=X⁢(D-λ~⁢I)-1⁢X-1⁢r

since A is diagonalizable; taking the p-norm (http://planetmath.org/VectorPnorm) of both sides, we obtain:

1 = ∥u~∥p
= ∥X⁢(D-λ~⁢I)-1⁢X-1⁢r∥p≤∥X∥p⁢∥(D-λ~⁢I)-1∥p⁢∥X-1∥p⁢∥r∥p
= κp⁢(X)⁢∥(D-λ~⁢I)-1∥p⁢∥r∥p.

But, since (D-λ~⁢I)-1 is a diagonal matrix, the p-norm is easily computed, and yields:

∥(D-λ~⁢I)-1∥p=max∥x∥p≠0⁡∥(D-λ~⁢I)-1⁢x∥p∥x∥p=maxλ∈σ⁢(A)⁡1|λ-λ~|=1minλ∈σ⁢(A)⁡|λ-λ~|

whence:

minλ∈σ⁢(A)⁡|λ-λ~|≤κp⁢(X)⁢||r||p.
