Schur decomposition, proof of


The columns of the unitary matrix Q in Schur’s decomposition theorem form an orthonormal basis of ℂn. The matrix A takes the upper-triangular form D+N on this basis. Conversely, if v1,…,vn is an orthonormal basis for which A is of this form then the matrix Q with vi as its i-th column satisfies the theorem.

To find such a basis we proceed by induction on n. For n=1 we can simply take Q=1. If n>1 then let v∈ℂn be an eigenvector of A of unit length and let V=v⟂ be its orthogonal complement. If π denotes the orthogonal projection onto the line spanned by v then (1-π)⁢A maps V into V.

By induction there is an orthonormal basis v2,…,vn of V for which (1-π)⁢A takes the desired form on V. Now A=π⁢A+(1-π)⁢A so A⁢vi≡(1-π)⁢A⁢vi(modv) for i∈{2,…,n}. Then v,v2,…,vn can be used as a basis for the Schur decomposition on ℂn.
