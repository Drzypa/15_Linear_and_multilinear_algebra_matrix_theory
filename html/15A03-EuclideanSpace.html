Euclidean space


1 Definition

Euclidean n-space is a metric space (E,d) with the property that the group of isometries is transitive and is isomorphic to an n-dimensional Euclidean vector space. To be more precise, we are saying that there exists an n-dimensional Euclidean vector space V with inner product ⟨⋅,⋅⟩ and a mapping

+:E×V→E

such that the following hold:

  1. 1.

    For all x,y∈E there exists a unique u∈V satisfying

    y=x+u,d⁢(x,y)2=⟨u,u⟩,
  2. 2.

    For all x,y∈E and all u∈V we have

    d⁢(x+u,y+u)=d⁢(x,y).
  3. 3.

    For all x∈E and all u,v∈V we have

    (x+u)+v=x+(u+v).

Putting it more succinctly: V acts transitively and effectively on E by isometries.

Remarks.

  • •

    The difference between Euclidean space and a Euclidean vector space is one of loss of structure. Euclidean space is a Euclidean vector space that has “forgotten” its origin.

  • •

    A 2-dimensional Euclidean space is often called a Euclidean plane.
