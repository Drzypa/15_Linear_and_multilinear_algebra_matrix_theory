eigenvalues of an involution


Proof. For the first claim suppose λ is an eigenvalue corresponding to an eigenvector x of A. That is, A⁢x=λ⁢x. Then A2⁢x=λ⁢A⁢x, so x=λ2⁢x. As an eigenvector, x is non-zero, and λ=±1. Now property (1) follows since the determinant is the product of the eigenvalues. For property (2), suppose that A-λ⁢I=-λ⁢A⁢(A-1/λ⁢I), where A and λ are as above. Taking the determinant of both sides, and using part (1), and the properties of the determinant, yields

det⁡(A-λ⁢I)=±λn⁢det⁡(A-1λ⁢I).

Property (2) follows. □
