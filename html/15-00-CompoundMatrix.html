compound matrix


Suppose that A is an m×n matrix with entries from a field F and 1≤r≤min⁡(m,n). The rt⁢h compound matrix or rt⁢h of A is the (mr)×(nr) matrix whose entries are detA[α,β]), α∈Qr,m and β∈Qr,n, arranged in lexicographic order and we use submatrix notation. The notation for this matrix is Cr⁢(A).

Properties

  1. 1.

    Cr⁢(A⁢B)=Cr⁢(A)⁢Cr⁢(B) when r is less than or equal to the number of rows or columns of A and B

  2. 2.

    If A is nonsingular, the Cr⁢(A)-1=Cr⁢(A-1).

  3. 3.

    If A has complex entries, then Cr⁢(A*)=(Cr⁢(A))*.

  4. 4.

    Cr⁢(AT)=(Cr⁢(A))T

  5. 5.

    Cr⁢(A¯)=Cr⁢(A)¯

  6. 6.

    For any k∈F Cr⁢(k⁢A)=kr⁢Cr⁢(A)

  7. 7.

    Cr⁢(In)=I(nr)

  8. 8.

    det⁡(Cr⁢(A))=det⁡(A)(n-1r-1) (Sylvester — Franke theorem)
