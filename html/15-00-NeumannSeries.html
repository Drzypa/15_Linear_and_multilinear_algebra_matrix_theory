Neumann series


If A is a square matrix, ∥A∥<1, then I-A is nonsingular and (I-A)-1=I+A+A2+⋯=∑k=0∞Ak. This is the Neumann series.
It provides approximations of (I-A)-1 when A has entries of small magnitude. For example, a first-order approximation is (I-A)-1≈I+A.
It is obvious that this is a generalization of the geometric series.
