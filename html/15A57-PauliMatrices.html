Pauli matrices


The Pauli matrices are a set of three Hermitian, unitary matrices used by Wolfgang Pauli in his theory of quantum-mechanical spin. They are given by:

σ1 =(0110)
σ2 =(0-ii0)
σ3 =(100-1)

They satisfy the following commutation and anticommutation identities:

[σi,σj] =2⁢i⁢ϵi⁢j⁢k⁢σk⁢where ϵi⁢j⁢k is the Levi-Civita symbol
{σi,σj} =2⁢𝐈⁢δi⁢j⁢where 𝐈 is the identity matrix and δi⁢j is the Kronecker delta

0.1 Delta notation

With the identity matrix I, the Pauli matrices form a group. When combined in this way, they are often given the symbols δi, as follows:

δ0 =(1001)
δ1 =(0110)
δ2 =(0-ii0)
δ3 =(100-1)

This choice is useful when writing the Dirac matrices.
