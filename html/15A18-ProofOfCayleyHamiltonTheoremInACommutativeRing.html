proof of Cayley-Hamilton theorem in a commutative ring


Let R be a commutative ring with identity and let A be an order n matrix with elements from R⁢[x]. For example, if A is (x2+2⁢x7⁢x2x+15)

then we can also associate with A the following polynomial having matrix coefficents:

Aσ=[01 05]+[21 00]⁢x+[10 70]⁢x2.

In this way we have a mapping A⟶Aσ which is an isomorphism of the rings Mn⁢(R⁢[x]) and Mn⁢(R)⁢[x].

Now let A∈Mn⁢(R) and consider the characteristic polynomial of A: pA⁢(x)=det⁡(x⁢I-A), which is a monic polynomial of degree n with coefficients in R. Using a property of the adjugate matrix we have

(x⁢I-A)⁢adj⁡(x⁢I-A)=pA⁢(x)⁢I.

Now view this as an equation in Mn⁢(R)⁢[x]. It says that x⁢I-A is a left factor of pA⁢(x). So by the factor theorem, the left hand value of pA⁢(x) at x=A is 0. The coefficients of pA⁢(x) have the form c⁢I, for c∈R, so they commute with A. Therefore right and left hand values are the same.
