nilpotent transformation


A linear transformation N:U→U is called nilpotent if there exists a k∈ℕ such that

Nk=0.

A nilpotent transformation naturally determines a flag of subspaces

{0}⊂ker⁡N1⊂ker⁡N2⊂…⊂ker⁡Nk-1⊂ker⁡Nk=U

and a signature

0=n0<n1<n2<…<nk-1<nk=dim⁡U,ni=dim⁡ker⁡Ni.

The signature is governed by the following constraint, and characterizes N up to linear isomorphism.

Proposition 1

A sequence of increasing natural numbers is the signature of a nil-potent transformation if and only if

nj+1-nj≤nj-nj-1

for all j=1,…,k-1. Equivalently, there exists a basis of U such that the matrix of N relative to this basis is block diagonal

(N100…00N20…000N3…0⋮⋮⋮⋱⋮000…Nk),

with each of the blocks having the form

Ni=(010…00001…00⋮⋮⋮⋱⋮000…10000…01000…00)

Letting di denote the number of blocks of size i, the signature of N is given by

ni=ni-1+di+di+1+…+dk,i=1,…,k
