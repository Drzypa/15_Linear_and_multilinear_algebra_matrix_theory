derivative of homogeneous function


Theorem 1.

Suppose f:Rn→Rm is a differentiable positively homogeneous function of degree r. Then ∂⁡f∂⁡xi is a positively homogeneous function of degree r-1.

Proof.

By considering component functions if necessary, we can assume that m=1. For λ∈ℝ, let Mλ be the multiplication map,

Mλ:ℝn → ℝn
v ↦ λ⁢v.

For λ>0 and v∈ℝn, we have

∂⁡f∂⁡xi⁢(λ⁢v) = ∂⁡(f∘Mλ∘M1/λ)∂⁡xi⁢(λ⁢v)
= ∑l=1n∂⁡(f∘Mλ)∂⁡xl⁢(v)⁢∂(x↦x/λ)l∂⁡xi⁢(λ⁢v)
= ∂⁡(f∘Mλ)∂⁡xi⁢(v)⁢1λ
= λr-1⁢∂⁡f∂⁡xi⁢(v)

as claimed. ∎
