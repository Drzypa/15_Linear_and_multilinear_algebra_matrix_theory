spark of a matrix


Let 𝔽 be a field, and let m,n∈ℕ∖{0}. Consider an m×n matrix A whose entries belong to 𝔽. Let C1,…,Cn∈𝔽m be the columns of A.

Definition 1

The spark of the matrix A is defined to be the infimum of the set of all positive integers k with the property that

∃j1,…,jk∈{1,…,n}:{j1<…<jk,𝐶𝑗1⁢, …, C𝑗𝑘⁢a⁢r⁢e⁢l⁢i⁢n⁢e⁢a⁢r⁢l⁢y⁢d⁢e⁢p⁢e⁢n⁢d⁢e⁢n⁢t⁢(o⁢v⁢e⁢r⁢𝔽).

The notion of spark of a matrix was introduced by Donoho and Elad in [1]. It is strictly related to Compressed Sensing. The word “spark” comes from a verbal fusion of “sparse” and “rank”.

Remark 2

The following properties hold true:
(i) spark⁢(A)∈{1,…,n}∪{+∞}, (ii) spark⁢(A)=+∞⇔rank⁢(A)=n, (iii) spark⁢(A)=1 if and only if the matrix A has a zero column, (iv) if spark⁢(A)≠+∞, then spark⁢(A)≤rank⁢(A)+1.
Moreover, spark⁢(A) coincides with the infimum of the set {∥x∥0:x∈Fn∖{0},A⁢x=0}, where ∥x∥0 is the Hamming weight of x and 0 stands for the appropriate zero vectors.
