conductor of a vector


Let k be a field, V a vector space, T:V→V a linear transformation, and W a T-invariant subspace of V. Let x∈V. The T-conductor of x in W is the set ST⁢(x,W) containing all polynomials g∈k⁢[X] such that g⁢(T)⁢x∈W. It happens to be that this set is an ideal of the polynomial ring. We also use the term T-conductor of x in W to refer to the generator of such ideal.

In the special case W={0}, the T-conductor is called T-annihilator of x. Another way to define the T-conductor of x in W is by saying that it is a monic polynomial p of lowest degree such that p⁢(T)⁢x∈W. Of course this polynomial happens to be unique. So the T-annihilator of x is the monic polynomial mx of lowest degree such that mx⁢(T)⁢x=0.
