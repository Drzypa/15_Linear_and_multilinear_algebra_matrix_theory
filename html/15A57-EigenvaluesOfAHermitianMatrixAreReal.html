eigenvalues of a Hermitian matrix are real


The eigenvalues of a Hermitian (or self-adjoint) matrix are real.

Proof.

Suppose λ is an eigenvalue of the self-adjoint matrix A with non-zero eigenvector v. Then A⁢v=λ⁢v.

λ∗⁢vH⁢v=(λ⁢v)H⁢v=(A⁢v)H⁢v=vH⁢AH⁢v=vH⁢A⁢v=vH⁢λ⁢v=λ⁢vH⁢v

Since v is non-zero by assumption, vH⁢v is non-zero as well and so λ*=λ, meaning that λ is real. ∎
